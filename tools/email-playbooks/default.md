# Default

A plain, specific pitch that earns the reply on the strength of the offer. Every other email playbook is a variation of it.

## The play

Say the offer well and get out.

Open on them, not you: something you noticed about them, or a real reason for reaching out, then a question about their world. Keep it plain and short, "you" over "we", under ~50 words. Name the mechanism only when the outcome reads skeptical. The CTA is value-based: an asset, an intro, a breakdown you can send, never "book a call". Follow-ups rephrase or add value, and never re-introduce.

## Needs

- A clear offer with one concrete, quantified outcome.
- One proof point (a matched result) or a risk reversal (a guarantee).
- A real reason to reach out, or true knowledge of this person's world, that the opener can acknowledge: something you noticed, a trigger, something specific to them.
- Base personalization: `{{first_name}}`, `{{company_name}}`.

## Subject lines

Simple and internal, the kind a colleague sends. Mix natural and outcome.

- Quick question {{first_name}}
- {{company_name}} x {us}
- 8 calls in 60 days or you don't pay

## Touches

Two touches.

- **Touch 1** - the pitch. 2 to 4 variants, depending on the size of the list.
- **Touch 2** - the follow-up, providing extra value. 2 variants.

## Template

**Touch 1**

> Hey {{first_name}},
>
> {opener: something you noticed about them, or a real reason for reaching out}
>
> We {the offer, stated with the number}.
>
> {a numbered proof, or a heavy risk reversal, on its own line}.
>
> {a value-based CTA: an asset, an intro, or a breakdown you can send}

**Touch 2** - the follow-up, body only, threads under Touch 1

> {a new angle or a piece of value} {a lighter re-ask}

## Examples

**Lead-gen (our own offer):**

> Hey {{first_name}},
>
> Saw {{company_name}} is hiring on the sales side, so pipeline's probably on your mind.
>
> We book 15 to 20 qualified sales calls a month for B2B teams.
>
> You only pay per call that actually shows up.
>
> I can send the exact play we'd run for {{company_name}}, no call needed. Want it?

**B2B SaaS (MovePlnr):**

> Hey {{first_name}},
>
> Was looking up movers in {{city}} and {{company_name}} kept coming up.
>
> When a lead comes in while you're out on a job, does it get quoted that day, or does it sit? Every one that sits is a booked move you lose.
>
> Cactus fixed that side first and tripled its moves, 372 to over 1,000 a month, on the way from $2M to $7M.
>
> Sam built the system running his own company. Want me to introduce you to him?
