When Sam Grinshpon launched Cactus Moving in Calgary, growth came fast. Phones rang nonstop, trucks were booked solid, and new jobs piled up. But that momentum came with operational friction.

Before MovePlnr, the day-to-day reality looked like a typical moving company juggling act. Leads arrived via website and calls, then landed in a shared inbox where ownership was unclear and timing was luck. Estimates lived in spreadsheets that differed by estimator, so pricing drifted and customers got inconsistent experiences.

Schedules lived in Google Calendar with no guardrails. Anyone could drag and drop an event, which meant well-intentioned changes sometimes caused double bookings, missed arrival windows, and awkward follow-up calls to customers. [Invoicing](https://moveplnr.com/accounting-payments) sat in a separate app altogether, creating duplicate entry, mismatched line items, and time-consuming reconciliation.

That patchwork held while the team was small. As hiring accelerated and Cactus looked beyond Calgary, it became obvious that a moving business isn't just trucks and crews—it's a synchronized, data-informed operation. Accountability was the missing piece: who touched which job, who promised which date, who changed which assignment, and whether margins held up across services and cities.
