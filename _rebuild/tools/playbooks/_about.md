# Playbooks

A playbook is a named, reusable play a copywriter runs: a way to write, a modifier, or an intent-driven campaign. It is a recipe, not a skill; the copywriter is the engine and the playbook is what it runs. A play lives here once it is reusable enough to stand on its own; a one-off angle stays in the copy.

## Properties (frontmatter)

- `type`: playbook
- `vertical`: the vertical that runs it (copy, list-building, ...)
- `channel`: email, linkedin, or both
- `signal`: the intent signal it runs on, or none (none means it works on any list, a way to write rather than a triggered play)
- `sources`: the sourcing move it needs, or standard (a cross-ref to list-building)

## Body

- A line or two of purpose: what it is and when to use it.
- `## The play`: the actual craft, the hook, the moves, example phrasings, the pitfalls. Enough to run it well, not a word more.
