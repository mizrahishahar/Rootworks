# Conversation

They wrote back with a question, objection, or logistics - not a plain yes. Answer with value, then the same natural-next-step mechanic.

## When
Any interested reply that carries a question or objection rather than a yes to a time. The sharp, most-common situation after First reply.

## The play
1. **Answer their exact question** with a short, personalised value answer. This is the fulfillment.
2. **Do not fulfill the test run or live run** - that is what the call is for.
3. **Do not push the lead magnet** unless they asked for it or said yes to it.
4. Then the same mechanic as First reply: a natural reason the call is the next step, two windows in their timezone, and an invitation to name their own time.

## Never
Essay answers that satisfy them over email; answering the situation in the abstract instead of their actual sentence; arguing an objection - reframe it, never debate it.

## Example
They asked "does this work if we already run HubSpot?":

> Yeah - it sits on top of HubSpot rather than replacing it, so your reps keep working where they already are and the quote just goes out faster. The part worth seeing is what that speed does to close rate on the jobs already in your pipeline.
>
> Sam maps that onto your exact setup on the call. He's open Thursday 11am or Friday 3pm ET - which works? Or send a time that suits and I'll set it up.

Answers the exact sentence with value, holds the real demo for the call, reframes toward the outcome, then the natural next step.
