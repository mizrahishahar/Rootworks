# The Opening Move

The first DM. One job: earn a reply from the right person. Because the offer is a no-brainer, you pitch in message one. The craft is making a no-brainer offer land on a real human who has never heard of you.

## The shape

Four beats, short, written like one person typing to another:

1. **Personalized hook**: one specific, true thing about them, pulled from real research. Their recent post, their raise, a hiring signal, their positioning in their own words. Not flattery. A signal that you actually looked.
2. **The offer, stated plainly**: you book qualified demo calls, performance-based, they pay only per meeting that happens. The risk reversal is the hook; surface it, do not bury it.
3. **The value layer** (when it earns its place): an insight about how they would present their offer to cold traffic, or where their reachable demand really sits. This is where you deploy cold-offer-generator. It turns the DM from a pitch into a gift.
4. **The CTA on the pitch**: the close is a soft, low-friction ask to hear the pitch out, not a qualifying question. "If that sounds relevant, happy to tell you more. Open to it?" The yes or no is easy to answer, and a yes opens the door to the full pitch.

## Rules

- Hyper-personalized or it does not send. If a line could sit on a hundred other DMs, it is wrong.
- Pitch-first, but never a hard-pitch blast. The personalization and the value layer are what separate you from the DM spam they ignore.
- Restrained, not eager. You are not impressed easily. You have done this before.
- Hebrew for Israeli founders, as a direct message, never a connection-request note.
- The close is a CTA on the pitch, not a demand. Not a hard "just say yes and I will start" either; a soft "open to hearing more?" invites a yes or no either way and opens the door to the pitch.
- Never an em dash.

## The value layer, in practice

The strongest opening DMs carry an insight the prospect recognizes as true about their own business, framed as your read so it is confident without being presumptuous. Examples of the move from real threads:

- Atlas Invest: "your engine lets you fund the small deals big lenders ignore, so your ceiling is not underwriting, it is deal flow. That is the part I fill."
- Eikona: "your buyer is one of the most targetable I have seen. After the seed, the thing that moves the needle is demo pipeline, and this is the cheapest channel to fill it."

Build these with cold-offer-generator. The insight is the gift; the offer is the ask; the CTA is the door.
