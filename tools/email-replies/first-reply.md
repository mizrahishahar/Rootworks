# First reply

They answered the cold email with interest ("sure, send it over", "sounds interesting"). Fulfill, then make the meeting the natural next step.

## When
The first genuine, interested reply to the sequence. Not a question or objection (that is Conversation), not a plain yes to a time (that is Meeting booked).

## The play
1. **Fulfill** what was promised in line one - the asset, or the value itself.
2. **Outcome, always** - tied to the exact angle they replied to, contextual, never generic. Add fresh value on it, never restate the thread.
3. **The meeting section, assertive and unsqueezed:** state the call as the natural next step ("next step is getting you with Sam so he maps it onto how you actually run"), never pitch it as an idea. Then two specific times in their timezone with a real question as the ask ("Which one works for you?"). Then invite their own time on its own line ("If neither does, just send me a time that suits and I'll set it up.").

## Never
Over-answer so the email satisfies them instead of the call; a calendar link; an invented duration; selling the call down.

## Example
An owner replied yes to the walkthrough; a teammate runs a tool he likes (a latent already-set objection):

> Here you go, John: {loom link}
>
> Watch it with one number in mind: how many jobs a month you would win if every lead got an instant quote before the other guy called back. For a six-crew LA shop that is the gap between full trucks and paid days slipping by.
>
> That is what Sam makes real on the call - he ran his own moving company on this and took it from $2M to over $7M. Even if your setup runs fine today, this is about the revenue sitting right next to it.
>
> Next step is getting you with Sam so he maps this onto how your shop actually runs. He's got Monday at 1pm or Tuesday at 2pm PT. Which one works for you?
>
> If neither does, just send me a time that suits and I'll set it up.

Fulfills in line one; the reason to call is money he is leaving on the table, tied to his real world; "even if your setup runs fine" neutralises the happy-with-current-tool objection without naming it; the meeting is stated as the next step, two times, a real question, own-time invite on its own line. No duration, no link.
