# Piper AI — Cold Traffic Offer

## ICP (locked)
US General Contractors, CM-at-Risk, and design-build firms, $50M–$500M (sweet spot $100M–$300M), 50–500 employees, with a named preconstruction or estimating department, bidding institutional and commercial vertical work. Signers/recommenders: VP or Director of Preconstruction, Chief Estimator, VP/Director of BD; the owner/President at smaller firms.

## The offer (one proposition)

> **Piper turns your full bid package, drawings, specs, contracts, addenda, into a decision-ready, source-cited brief on risks, scope gaps, schedule, and pricing logic in hours instead of weeks, so your team pursues and wins more bids without adding headcount, and you can watch it run on one of your own live RFPs before you commit to anything.**

Two emphases (same offer):
- **Precon Operators** lead with the workflow/time pain: stop reading 800-page packages by hand, go/no-go in 30 minutes, catch the scope gap before bid day not after award.
- **Business Leaders** lead with the business outcome: pursue more bids and protect margin without adding headcount, win the right work defensibly.

- Claim: weeks of manual bid review compressed to hours, every finding cited to its source page, no hallucinations.
- Evidence: real paying-client results from the onboarding form (anonymized firm type + role + number), usable as social proof per Piper's note. No formal US case studies with quantified ROI yet (expected 60–90 days); also leaning on the live-on-your-own-RFP proof and the citation differentiator.
- Guarantee: implicit/performance, via the POV, no long-term commitment until the output proves itself on their work.

## Differentiation (the wedge)
The buyer is solution-aware and already burned by generic AI (Copilot/ChatGPT gave confident wrong answers, so they stopped trusting it on real bids). Differentiation:
- Trust / no hallucination, every output cited to the exact source page (the precise failure mode that killed generic AI for them). Primary axis: less risk.
- Faster, hours not weeks; learns from their own past bids; no implementation.
- Scale without headcount, pursue more bids than the team could analyze by hand.

## Why it works on cold traffic
Demand-capture, not generation: they already want the outcome and have tried and failed with generic AI. The offer differentiates inside a category they feel daily, and the risk reversal (watch it work on your own live RFP, commit nothing) is the strongest possible for a conservative, skeptical buyer.

## CTA decision (resolved — 2026-06-14 onboarding call)
The whole account runs on one CTA pool, no document is ever requested from the prospect (the friction Nikol flagged: RFPs run 9 to 7,000+ pages, often sensitive, rarely shared cold):
- **Free RFP-run** — framed as value the prospect receives, not a demo. We source and run it on us, nothing for them to dig up, and go through what comes back (conversation kept soft, not pitched as a call).
- **Two-month no-commitment trial** — run it on a few of their own live pursuits for a month or two before locking into anything long-term (the POV). It earns the next step or it doesn't.
- **Loom walkthrough** — the lower-intent "want me to send a quick walkthrough" option.
- **Referral** — the "who's the best person for this" ask, Email 3 only.
