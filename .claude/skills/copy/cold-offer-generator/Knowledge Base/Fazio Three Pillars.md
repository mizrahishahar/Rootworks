# Fazio Three Pillars

The Daniel Fazio framework for assessing whether a cold-traffic offer can work on a stranger. Every pillar must be present — or explicitly compensated for. The three pillars together answer the only question that matters to a cold prospect: *is this worth my response?*

---

## Pillar 1 — New Money

The offer positions as generating **new** revenue or result — not optimizing what the prospect already has.

- New Money is **role-specific**. Define it relative to the primary ICP role.
  - Founder / CEO: new booked calls, new pipeline, new clients
  - Marketing Director: new channel reach, new audience, new attributed revenue
  - Sales Lead: new SQLs, new closed deals
  - Operator: new throughput, new capacity
- If New Money cannot be established for the primary role, the offer has near-zero cold traffic viability.
- **Word trickery** — implying New Money through framing when delivery does not perfectly produce it. Acceptable when it is the only viable path. Must be flagged.

---

## Pillar 2 — Done For You

The prospect lifts nothing. Real humans (or AI under operator supervision) handle the work entirely.

- **Never:** "coaching," "consulting," "we teach you," "here's the playbook"
- **Always:** concrete deliverables handled entirely by the service provider
- DFY is what separates a $10K offer from a $1K offer. The cold prospect is buying *the transfer of work off their plate*, not the knowledge.

---

## Pillar 3 — Risk Reversal

Removes the perceived risk of losing money on the offer.

- **Mechanisms** (least risky for buyer → most):
  - **Performance basis** — pay-per-result. Pay-per-call, pay-per-lead, pay-per-client signed. Zero financial risk.
  - **Money-back guarantee** — refund if specific result is not hit. Small residual risk that the operator does not honor it.
  - **Implicit guarantee** — overwhelming evidence carries the promise; "guarantee" word not needed. Only viable with major authority.

- **Specificity = strength.**
  - Weak: "Results or your money back."
  - Strong: "8 booked calls in 60 days or a full refund."

- **Cold-traffic-facing language only.** The marketable shape, not the legal terms. Stipulations and refund conditions live in the contract.

---

## Pillar Compensation

If one pillar is weak or absent, over-index on the other two.

| Weak Pillar | Response |
|---|---|
| New Money | Make DFY and guarantee language exceptionally strong. Push DFY scope to the maximum the operator can deliver. |
| Done For You | Make New Money outcome and guarantee carry the offer. Quantify the outcome aggressively. |
| Risk Reversal | Make New Money and DFY undeniable. Lean heavily on case studies and concept proof. |

Two or more pillars weak = **not viable on cold traffic.** Restructure the offer before proceeding — change scope, change role targeting, or change pricing model.

---

## The viability call

For every offer, assess all three pillars and produce one of three outcomes:

- **Strong** — all three pillars clearly present. Offer is cold-traffic ready.
- **Compensatable** — one pillar weak or absent; the other two can carry it with explicit over-indexing. Offer is cold-traffic viable with intentional framing.
- **Weak** — two or more pillars missing or fundamentally weak. Offer is not viable on cold traffic. Flag the structural change needed.

The cold offer is built with this assessment in mind. If compensation is required, the offer's wording must explicitly over-index — not hide the weakness.
