# Piper AI: Cold Traffic Offer

The proposition that goes into the D100 campaign aimed at US pre-construction leaders. Built from Piper's own positioning, ICP confirmed by Roi on the May 10 sales call, and public testimonials on piper-ai.com. No fabricated proof, results, or guarantee terms.

---

## 1. Cold Traffic Offer

> *Cut bid-package review from weeks to hours and protect margin on every pursuit. Piper ingests your full RFP (drawings, specs, contracts, addenda) and gives your pre-con team a decision-ready brief on risks, gaps, and pricing logic, with every finding linked to the source page. Bring us a live bid package and we'll show you, on your project, what we surface.*

**Headline variants** (same offer, different cold-traffic phrasings for testing):

1. *Stop losing margin to blind spots buried in 1,000-page bid packages. Piper is the AI analyst that reviews drawings, specs, and addenda in hours, with every risk and gap linked to its source. Bring us your next live RFP and we'll run it for you, no commitment.*

2. *Win the right work, defensibly. Piper turns a full bid package into a source-linked risk and pricing brief in hours so your estimators stop recycling templates and start bidding on what's actually in the package. See it on a live pursuit of yours.*

3. *Your next bid is sitting in 800 pages of drawings, specs, and addenda. Piper reads all of it in hours and hands your pre-con team a decision-ready brief on what to bid, how to price, and why. We'll prove it on a live RFP of yours, free.*

## 2. Pillar Assessment

| Pillar | Rating | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **New Money** | Strong | "Win more bid revenue" is Piper's core promise. For a VP Pre-Construction or Chief Estimator, every won bid is a multi-million-dollar project. Margin protection on a single bid often dwarfs the entire annual contract price. This is the most direct line to new wealth a pre-con leader has. |
| **Done For You** | Compensatable (word trickery) | Piper is software, not a service. The DFY pillar is filled by framing the AI as "the analyst doing the heavy lifting." Piper's own copy supports this: *"Piper acts like an autonomous analyst, doing the heavy lifting"* (Pini Liberman testimonial). The pre-con team does not learn a tool, they receive a brief. |
| **Risk Reversal** | Strong (at the offer level) | No money-back guarantee on the annual contract, and that is correct for cold-traffic shape: the risk reversal sits at the demo step. The free live-bid-package analysis means the prospect risks nothing to see the value on their own work. Zero financial commitment to find out if Piper is worth $20K–$70K. |

**Verdict.** Shippable to cold copy as-is. New Money is the strongest pillar and should lead. The DFY framing is honest, not invented (Piper's own positioning supports it). The risk reversal mechanism is the *free live-bid analysis*, not a money-back guarantee, and that is the right shape for a cold-traffic SaaS demo offer at this price tier. The offer does not need a fabricated outcome number to work, because the prospect can see the output on their own bid package before any commitment. Strong cold-traffic viability.

## 3. ICP

### Company profile

| Field               | Value                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| ------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Vertical / industry | US construction, civil/heavy construction, and engineering firms that respond to RFPs. General contractors, design-builders, MEP contractors, specialty trades, and consulting/engineering firms running pre-construction or proposal pursuits. |
| Stage               | Established. Operating, profitable, actively bidding. Not pre-revenue, not enterprise giants. The middle of the US construction market where pre-con bottlenecks are felt but enterprise procurement layers do not slow purchase.               |
| Size                | Annual revenue **$20M–$200M**. Pre-construction teams typically 3 to 15 people.                                                                                                                                                                 |
| Geography           | United States.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |

**Why this company.** A $20M to $200M US construction or engineering firm lives or dies on its win rate against the bids it pursues. They bid often enough that pre-construction review is a constant bottleneck, but they are not large enough to have a dedicated bid-intelligence team or a six-figure software budget already committed. They feel every blind spot directly in margin. They have seen one or two bids in the past year where a buried clause or missed addendum cost them the project or compressed the margin to a sliver. They know the problem is real, they have already tried to solve it with more estimators or longer hours, and they are at the size where a $20K–$70K annual contract reads as a real but defensible cost if it protects even one pursuit per year. This is the band where the math on Piper closes itself.

| Why this role buys this | This is the person whose job *is* turning bid packages into pursuit decisions. Every hour their team spends manually reading drawings is an hour not spent qualifying the next pursuit. Every blind spot is their fault upstream when the project loses margin in execution. Buying Piper is the single most defensible software purchase a pre-con leader can make to their President: faster turnarounds, fewer misses, more bids submitted, better win rate. The dimension of value (won bids, protected margin) is the exact dimension they are already measured on. |

**Why this person.** A VP of Pre-Construction at a $50M GC has the same problem every Monday morning: a 600-page bid package landed Friday afternoon, his team has eight days to submit, two of his three estimators are already buried in another pursuit, and he knows there are addenda and spec details that will not get read closely enough. His daily pain is volume. His expensive pain is the bid they lost last quarter because nobody caught an exclusion clause buried in section 7. His status pain is that his President keeps asking why their win rate is sliding, and he does not have an answer that is not "we need more people." His trigger pain is the moment a new RFP lands and he can already see the team is going to half-read it. Piper does not replace his team. It removes the half-read problem. For someone whose name is on every lost pursuit, that is the offer that gets the email opened.

### Buying signals

- **External:**
  - Recent or active pursuit of a large RFP (LinkedIn posts, press releases announcing project wins/bids).
  - Recent hire of an estimator, pre-con manager, or VP Pre-Con (signals capacity strain or restructuring).
  - Announcement of expansion into a new region or project type (new bid territory = new bid-package complexity).
  - Visible bid losses or public commentary from leadership about win rate or margin pressure.
- **Internal:**
  - Pre-con team size has not scaled with bid volume.
  - Firm has recently adopted other AI or construction-tech tools (Procore, Autodesk Construction Cloud, Building Connected, StructionSite). Signals openness to category.
  - Leadership is publicly active on LinkedIn around pre-construction, estimating efficiency, or construction-tech adoption.
