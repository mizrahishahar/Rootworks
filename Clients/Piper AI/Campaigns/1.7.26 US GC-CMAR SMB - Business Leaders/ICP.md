# ICP — Business Leaders (down-market)

**Campaign:** 1.7.26 US GC-CMAR SMB - Business Leaders

## Company
US General Contractors / CM-at-Risk / design-build, **$1-10M revenue** (the down-market tier parked from the 2026-06-30 build). Small, owner-operated shops. Usually no dedicated preconstruction department, at this size the owner is the estimator. US + Canada. Bidding real institutional, public, and commercial vertical work at a regional/local scale.

## Decision-maker
Owner / President / CEO / Founder / Partner / Principal. The one who decides which jobs the firm chases and how they get resourced. At this size that is the same person who runs the company and prices the bids. Persona call-outs do not earn their keep here (small firm), so the copy names the company and speaks owner-to-owner.

## Offer emphasis (down-market re-angle)
Same product, business-outcome framing for the owner: win more of the jobs you already bid, without hiring an estimator, and stop losing margin to scope gaps and risks you catch after the job is won instead of before. Believability leans on modest numbered proof (go/no-go from a full day to under 30 minutes; bid leveling from a full day to minutes) plus a light mechanism (reads the full package, cites every flag to the page). CTA = the free live-RFP-run ("want us to run one of your live RFPs through it, on us?"), framed as an offer, never claimed as already done. The POV/trial stays the deal-level risk reversal but is too heavy to use as the cold CTA.
