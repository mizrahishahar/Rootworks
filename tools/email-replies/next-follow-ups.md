# Next follow-ups

Still quiet after the first follow-up. The job is to get a response again. That is all. **No time slots.**

## When
Any nudge after the first follow-up has already gone unanswered. A third identical slot menu would read needy, so the move changes.

## The play
1. **Acknowledge (implicitly)** that they showed interest.
2. **Play hard on the outcome and the pain** the angle they replied to surfaced - this is where reading the whole thread as one conversation pays; we know exactly why they replied.
3. **Ask a completely relatable question** about that outcome or pain - one that, if answered, puts us back in the natural position to offer times again.

## Never
Slot menus; neediness; a bare nudge; a repeated angle; "quick one" (it reads as a scripted opener).

## Example
The CEO said yes, we pushed slots twice, then silence:

> Hey Will,
>
> I know it's busy mid-season. When a job request comes in off the site today, how fast does a quote usually go back out?
>
> Reason I ask - that gap is where Sam found most of the money when he ran his own shop.

Acknowledges he is slammed without apologising; no third slot menu; a relatable question on the exact outcome he replied to that, answered, puts us right back in position to offer times.
