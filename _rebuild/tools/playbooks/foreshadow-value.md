# Foreshadow-value

Pure curiosity: you tease a specific, valuable thing you have for them and let the reply come from wanting it. No pitch in the email at all, the offer waits for the reply or the call. Use when the value is real and specific enough to stand on its own, and a pitch would only get in its way.

## The play

- **Name the value, hold it back** — "made you a {{asset}}", or "spotted three things on {{company}}'s {{surface}} that are costing you {{outcome}}." Specific enough to be real, held back enough to require a reply.
- **No pitch** — do not say what you sell or ask for a call. The email's only job is to make them want the thing.
- **CTA** — the smallest possible: "want it?", "want me to send it over?"
- The value must be true and already in hand. Foreshadowing a thing you have not made is a bait-and-switch, and it shows the moment they reply.
