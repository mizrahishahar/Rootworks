## Section 2 — Your Offer

**What do you sell?**
An on-demand, AI-powered cloud infrastructure / DevOps team. An **AI agent that becomes your infrastructure owner**: it plugs into your **tech stack** (cloud, Git, ticketing, comms channels), **reads your cloud and never writes to it**, and builds a **"context lake"** that maps your whole infra/cloud layer in minutes. A human **Remote Deployed Engineer (RDE)** checks and signs off on every change before it ships. You scale output without scaling headcount.

**What outcomes do you create? (numbers + timeframe)** *[use as Sean provides; do not invent specifics]*
- Reduced infrastructure cost by **~45%**.
- Freed developer time **~8x**.
- Value **in minutes**, vs. the **3–6 months** it takes to ramp a hire.
- **~$2,500/mo** vs. a **~$200–250K/yr** hire (plus recruiting fees).
- Migrated a client off AWS corporate before deprecation.

**How do you achieve these outcomes? (features / mechanisms)**
- **Context lake** — the key differentiator. Auto-maps the entire infra + cloud layer, which is what enables day-one value instead of a months-long ramp.
- **Read-only cloud access** — secure by design; Dave never writes to your cloud.
- **Human-in-the-loop RDE** — a real engineer signs off on every change; the client's own approver (CTO / VP Eng / Dir DevOps) typically does a ~60-second final sign-off.
- **Integrations across the stack** — AWS/GCP/Azure, Git, ticketing, comms.

**What do you take off the client's plate?**
End-to-end **infrastructure ownership**: deployments, firefighting / incident response, CI/CD pipelines, Infrastructure-as-Code (Terraform / Pulumi), Kafka rebalancing, cloud resource management, cost + security cleanup, and migrations.

**What more could you take on (esp. bigger clients)?**
For larger teams, act as a **force multiplier** for a stretched in-house DevOps function (relieve the bottleneck rather than replace an owner). Specific premium / done-for-you tiers — **OPEN** (can't answer for them; ask Sean).

**What problems do clients come to you with? (their words)**
- *"Nobody actually owns the infrastructure, so it keeps pulling the team off the product."*
- *"Handling deployments and putting out fires whenever something breaks."*
- Developers forced into infra work they aren't expert at (**"plumbing and firefighting"**) — they hate it, it kills their focus, and they build things that break.
- Larger teams: **1–2 DevOps bottlenecking 45–60 engineers**, tickets piling up, big projects pushed.
