# Competitor-followers

The follow is the whole hook: they follow a competitor, a tool, or a category page, so they are already aware of the problem you solve. Soft intent, but a real pattern break.

## The play

Lead with the follow, offer the alternative.

Lead with the follow, framed as observation not surveillance, because a follow is public: "saw you follow {{page}}, looked at alternatives yet or still evaluating?" The offer is the alternative or the upgrade, carried by the proof that separates you from {{page}}. Exclude current and former employees before you send; reaching them is the embarrassing miss here.

## Needs

- A follower export of a chosen page, ICP-filtered.
- Current and former employees of the followed company excluded.
- Vars: `{{page}}`, `{{company_name}}`.

## Touches

Connection-gated.

- **Connection request** - blank.
- **Message 1** - the follow, noticed, and the alternative. 2 variants.
- **Message 2** - a soft compare, low commitment. 2 variants.

## Template

**Connection request** - blank

**Message 1** (on accept)

> Hey {{first_name}}, saw you follow {{page}}.
>
> Looked at alternatives yet, or still evaluating? We do {the offer}, {the one thing that separates us}.
>
> Open to a quick compare?

**Message 2** (follow-up)

> {a switch proof, or a soft low-commitment nudge}

## Examples

> Hey {{first_name}}, saw you're following {{page}}.
>
> Curious if you've looked at alternatives yet, or still evaluating? We help {{ICP}} {outcome}, usually {the differentiator}.
>
> Worth a quick look?
