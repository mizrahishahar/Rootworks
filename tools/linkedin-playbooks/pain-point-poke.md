# Pain-point-poke

No cushion. You lead with the sharpest SDLC pain, stated as a plain question they feel in their gut. Where permission-poke disarms first, this one trusts that the pain is real enough to earn the reply on its own. Best when the trigger is strong and the pain is specific: they are hiring for infra, so the hurt is not hypothetical, it is why the role is open. One honest question about the thing that actually costs them, and they answer because it is true.

## The play

Skip the wind-up, ask the painful question.

Connect blank. Open on the trigger in one line, then go straight at the cost, framed as a real question, never a claim: how much of the engineers' week goes to infra instead of shipping, who carries the deploys and the fires, what the on-call is doing to the team. Name the SDLC moment concretely, not "infra" in the abstract. Short, plain, human. When they name it, go deeper by question. Only once the pain is on the table do you offer the next step, soft. Aim at the owner of the outcome, never the recruiter.

## Needs

- A live trigger per lead (a hiring signal), screened as genuine.
- Expansion to the decision-maker by company size; recruiters and HR excluded.
- Vars: `{{first_name}}`, `{{company_name}}`. The trigger stays generic in copy, no exact-role token.

## Touches

Connection-gated.

- **Connection request** - blank.
- **Message 1** - the trigger in a line, then one sharp SDLC pain question. 2 variants.
- **Message 2** - stack the second-order cost, still a question. 2 variants.
- **Message 3** - name what we do, soft, with a real out. 2 variants.

## Template

**Connection request** - blank

**Message 1** (on accept)

> Hey {{first_name}}, saw you're hiring on the infra side.
>
> How much of your engineers' week is actually going to infra right now instead of shipping?

**Message 2** (follow-up)

> The deploys, the fires, the on-call at 2am, guessing it's adding up on the people you'd rather have building?

**Message 3** (follow-up)

> If it's handled, ignore me. If not, we take that whole load off the team so they get back to product. Want me to show you where it'd help most?

## Examples

Different sharp openers for Message 1, one per variant:

> Hey {{first_name}}, saw you're hiring on the infra side. Who's carrying the deploys and the fires until that seat's filled?

> Hey {{first_name}}, saw you're hiring on the infra side. Real question, how many of your engineers' hours a week are going to infra instead of the roadmap?
