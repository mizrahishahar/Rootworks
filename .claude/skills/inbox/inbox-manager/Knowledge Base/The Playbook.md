# The Playbook

Ten use cases, each carrying a **framework**: a proven way to work, not a script. Name where the thread sits, then work its framework with a brain — personalization, context, and coherence with the whole thread are what make the framework land; the framework without them is a template, and we do not write templates.

**The mindset over everything: non-needy.** We don't sell, and we don't need them. We want to help them achieve the outcome they responded to, and the way to do it is a call with our client. Every message breathes this.

## 1. First Reply
**Trigger:** they answered the cold email with interest ("sure, send it over", "sounds interesting").
**Job:** fulfill, then make the meeting the natural next step.
**Framework:**
1. Fulfill what was promised, line one (the asset, or the value itself).
2. Outcome, always — consistent with the exact message and angle they replied to. Contextual, never generic.
3. The meeting section is NOT squeezed to minimum words, and it is assertive: state the call as the natural next step ("next step is getting you with Sam so he maps it onto how you actually run"), never pitch it as an idea ("worth a call?"), then two specific times in their timezone with a real question as the ask ("Which one works for you?"), then invite their own time on its own line ("If neither does, just send me a time that suits and I'll set it up.").
**Never:** over-answer, link, duration, selling the call down.

## 2. Conversation
**Trigger:** they wrote back with a question, objection, or logistics — not a plain yes.
**Job:** answer with value, then the same natural-next-step mechanic.
**The booking-offer rules (this is the sharp part):**
1. Answer their exact question with a short, personalized value answer. This IS the fulfillment.
2. Do not fulfill on the test run / live run — that is what the call is for.
3. Do not push the lead magnet unless they asked for it or said yes to it.
4. Then the same mechanic as First Reply: a natural reason the call is the next step, two windows, and an invitation to name their own time.
**Never:** essay answers that satisfy them over email; answering the situation in the abstract instead of their actual sentence; arguing an objection (reframe it).

## 3. First Follow-up
**Trigger:** our reply with slots sits quiet ~2-3 business days.
**Job:** a simple, casual, human check-in. Less aggressive than the First Reply.
**Framework:**
1. Casual open, no corporate words — "Hey, wanted to see..." energy, short.
2. One/few genuinely personalized lines — we hold his brief; make it read like research was done.
3. The slot move: acknowledge time passed. If the earlier slot is gone: "the {day} slot isn't available anymore, but {new slot} opened up — tell me which works." If both passed: say time passed and offer two new slots. **Two slots, no invite-your-own-time this round.**
**Never:** recap the pitch, apologize, aggressive call push, brand/corporate words.

## 4. Next Follow-ups
**Trigger:** still quiet after the first follow-up.
**Job:** get a response again. That is all. **No time slots.**
**Framework:**
1. Acknowledge (implicitly) that they showed interest.
2. Play hard on the outcome and the pain the angle they replied to surfaced — this is where reading the whole thread as one conversation pays; we know exactly why they replied.
3. Ask a completely relatable question about that outcome/pain — a question that, if answered, puts us in the natural position to offer times again.
**Never:** slot menus, neediness, a bare nudge, a repeated angle.

## 5. Reply After Ghosting
**Trigger:** the prospect replies to one of the follow-ups.
**Job:** back to the machine — the personalized outcome paragraph and the natural next step.
**Framework:** answer what they said with value → outcome, personalized → natural reason the call is the next step → two windows → invite their own time. (Same mechanic as First Reply, grounded in the fuller thread.)

## 6. Defer / Not-now
**Trigger:** "check back in a month", "after the season."
**Job:** honor it, date it, stay warm, short. One or two lines. Never push a call onto a clear not-now.

## 7. Meeting Booked
**Trigger:** they picked a time or agreed to one.
**Job:** convey the meeting is set (never the word "locked" — banned) and start the value bridge.
**Framework:**
1. The invite is sent — ask them to accept it. That's the confirmation; never ask them to re-confirm the time.
2. "Until the meeting I'll share a few materials so you get the most out of it" — phrased to the real gap (today/tomorrow/next week), framed as for THEM, outcome in mind.
**Never:** re-asking the time, over-thanking, calendar link.

## 8. Pre-Call
**Trigger:** the gap between booking and the call.
**Job:** show-up protection through assets.
**Framework:** check the company's Assets file, pick the asset that fits this moment, and deploy it **in context**: tied to the outcome, personalized to their situation, with why it's worth their minute. Reminder rides inside the value. Morning-of touch always.
**Never:** a bare reminder, an asset dumped as homework, an asset that doesn't exist.

## 9. No-Show
**Trigger:** the call time passed and they didn't show.
**Job:** rebook, more assertive. Make it understood they no-showed and that we're not mad (indirectly), but our time deserves respect.
**Framework:** name that we missed each other → one reason it's still worth it (outcome) → **two specific times, no invite-your-own-time.** Non-needy is the whole message.
**Never:** guilt trips, "you missed", asking what happened, apologizing for them.

## 10. Cold Re-open
**Trigger:** a lead quiet for weeks, beyond the follow-up rhythm.
**Job:** restart the human thread. A simple nudge or a genuine question about their world. Never repeat the call ask.

## The Pre-Send Gate

The draft is written, then checked — every time, before it is shown:

1. Right use case, framework honored?
2. Outcome-attached and consistent with the angle they replied to?
3. Personalized to the person, not the segment?
4. Slots real (connector or Operator-given), in their timezone, and the slot rule of THIS use case followed?
5. Under the length bar, one idea, one CTA — and does it pass the read-aloud test, with the ask as a real question?
6. Nothing echoed from the thread, nothing invented, no banned words, non-needy throughout?
7. Signature: "Thanks," then the rep's name.

Any miss: rewrite silently, then show.
