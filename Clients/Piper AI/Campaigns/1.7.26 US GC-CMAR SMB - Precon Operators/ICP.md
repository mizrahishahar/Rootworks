## Decision-maker
The person who does the bid-package review and estimating: Chief Estimator, Estimator, VP/Director of Estimating or Preconstruction, Preconstruction Manager, and the hybrid roles this size produces (PM/Estimator, Estimating Admin, Preconstruction Assistant). At $1-10M they are usually the **whole estimating department**, not one of several. Note: a minority of tagged Operators here are junior/hybrid, the copy writes to "the person bid review lands on," which is true either way. They are often the champion, not the economic buyer, so the follow-up carries an email-tree to route to the owner.

## Offer emphasis (down-market re-angle)
Same product, workflow-relief framing for the lone estimator: get through more bids without a second estimator, and catch the risk you would miss under deadline. Believability leans on modest numbered proof (bid leveling from a full day to minutes; go/no-go from a full day to under 30 minutes) plus a light mechanism (turns a full package into a cited risk and scope brief in hours, every flag tied to its source page, the exact thing they stopped trusting generic AI for). CTA = the free live-RFP-run ("want us to run one of your live RFPs through it, on us?"), framed as an offer, never claimed as already done; follow-up carries an email-tree to the owner. The POV/trial stays the deal-level risk reversal, not the cold CTA.
