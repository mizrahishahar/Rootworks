# First follow-up

Our reply with slots sits quiet ~2-3 business days. A simple, casual, human check-in - less aggressive than the First reply.

## When
The first nudge after we offered times and heard nothing for two to three business days.

## The play
1. **Casual open, no corporate words** - "no reason to sit on this" energy, short.
2. **One or few genuinely personalised lines** - we hold his brief; make it read like research was done.
3. **The slot move:** acknowledge time passed. If the earlier slot is gone, drop it and re-anchor: "Thursday is no longer open, but Friday 2pm PT just did. Monday 1pm PT works too - tell me which fits." **Two slots, no invite-your-own-time this round.**

## Never
Recap the pitch; apologise; an aggressive call push; brand or corporate words.

## Example
A positive owner went quiet after slots were offered, one already passed:

> Hi Stephan,
>
> No reason to sit on this - the sooner Sam walks you through what he changed in his own shop, the sooner it is working for Popeye.
>
> Thursday is no longer open, but Friday 2pm PT just did. Monday 1pm PT works too. Tell me which fits and I'll set it up.

Assumes the meeting is happening and drives it sooner; quietly drops the dead Thursday and re-anchors two new times with no own-time invite; not needy, not apologetic.
