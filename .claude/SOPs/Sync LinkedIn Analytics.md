mode: Solo
description: Procedure to sync engagement and signal data from recently published LinkedIn posts back into the content tracker.
---

## When to do it

Weekly

## What we get

LinkedIn Analytics on Airtable

---
## Process

### 1. Find what needs syncing

In the content tracker, filter for posts with status `published` that have no metrics yet, or whose metrics have not been updated since the last sync date.

List them in order from oldest to newest.

### 2. Open LinkedIn for each post

For each post in the list:

- Go to LinkedIn → click the profile picture top-right → **Posts & activity**, or paste the post URL from the content tracker directly into the address bar
- Click **View analytics** below the post body

### 3. Capture per-post metrics

From the post analytics page, pull and record into the content tracker:

- **Impressions** — total views
- **Reactions / Likes** — total
- **Comments** — total
- **Reposts** — total
- **Engagement rate** — if displayed directly, otherwise let the tracker's formula compute it
- **Notable demographics** — only when a clear pattern (titles, companies, industries) jumps out worth noting in the Notes field

### 4. Capture inbound signals

These do not appear in LinkedIn's post analytics. Capture from memory, DM inbox, and the calendar:

- **DMs received** that referenced or were clearly prompted by this post
- **Calls booked** where the prospect mentioned the post
- **New followers worth flagging** that connected after engaging with the post

Note these in the DMs / Leads field and the Notes field of the tracker.

### 5. Subjective rating

Rate the post on a 1–5 scale based on whether it landed the way it was supposed to. This is the writer's judgment, separate from the algorithm's.

**Gate:** Every post since the last sync has all metric fields populated. Notes capture anything unusual or memorable.

### 6. Mark the sync

Record the sync date in a known place (a property on the tracker, a row in a sync log, or a daily-log line) so the next session knows where to pick up from.

### 7. Optional — broader profile metrics

When running this sync alongside a content review session, also pull from LinkedIn → Me → **Analytics**:

- **Profile views** for the period since the last sync
- **Search appearances** — who searched for the operator and found them
- **Total followers gained** in the period

These do not tie to specific posts but inform the overall trajectory of the channel.
