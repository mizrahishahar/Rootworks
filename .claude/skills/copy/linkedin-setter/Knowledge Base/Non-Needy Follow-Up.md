# Non-Needy Follow-Up

The prospect saw the pitch and did not reply. Silence is not rejection; it is part of the game. Each follow-up gives from a position of value, never "just checking in".

## The mindset

You do not need this specific person. Outcome independence is not a pose; it is the actual frame. A follow-up that smells of need repels; one that foreshadows value or hands over a genuine insight re-opens.

## The arsenal

Pick the move by reading where the thread sits. Never repeat the ask.

- **Value foreshadowing**: drop a fresh insight about their world that extends the first message, so replying becomes worth their time. Often built with cold-offer-generator.
- **The imagined-reaction line**: voice the reaction you imagine they will have when they see the offer. Playful, confident, disarming. ("This is how I picture you reacting when you see it.") Proven to book calls.
- **The tailored value-asset drop**: a real, made-for-them asset (a D100 doc, a short Gamma) that signals friendly-helper, no strings. Most of its job is the gesture, not the consumption.
- **The qualifying re-open**: a sharp, easy-to-answer question that reframes their bottleneck and invites a reply either way. ("My bet: this is your least-used channel. Am I right, or missing something?")
- **The clean exit**: the takeaway that removes all neediness. "If this is not for you right now, just say and I will stop. No hard feelings." It respects the silence and re-opens more often than another push.

## Rules

- Never the words "following up", "circling back", "touching base", "just checking in".
- Never repeat the ask. Re-open with value or a question, not the pitch again.
- Restrained enthusiasm, 4 to 6 out of 10. A peer, not a vendor chasing.
- One idea per touch. Short. Typed like a human, not marketed.
- The instant they reply, stop. Hand to the inbox-manager. The follow-up game is pre-reply only.
- Never an em dash.
