# Problem-mirror

No trigger. You picked them by hand because they are the fit, the narrowest, best-match accounts. So prove it. Open on a description of their world so specific it reads like a line from their own diary, and earn the "how did you know that" before you have said what you do. The mirror is the hook. Because the list is hand-picked and tight, the detail can be sharp, and sharp is what separates this from every other cold message they ignore.

## The play

Mirror their world, then ask.

Connect blank. Open not on us, but on their reality, one concrete, lived thing that is true of a company exactly like this: the stage they are at, the way the pain shows up for them. State it plainly, then turn it into a question they would want to answer, never a claim to accept, always with an out ("or have you got it covered?"). Never a category line that could sit on a homepage; a real moment. Name the mechanism only after the mirror lands, in one plain line, as the natural resolution. One proof, close to the pain you mirrored. The next step is offered soft and late, once the pain is named. One idea a message, written like a person talking.

## Needs

- A hand-picked, tightly-qualified list of best-match accounts (the narrow build).
- One lived pain that is true of this exact segment, sharp enough to name in detail.
- Vars: `{{first_name}}`, `{{company_name}}`.

## Touches

Connection-gated.

- **Connection request** - blank.
- **Message 1** - the mirror, as a plain observation turned into one question. 2 variants.
- **Message 2** - the mechanism as the resolution, one matched proof. 2 variants.
- **Message 3** - a soft, unattached close with a real out. 2 variants.

## Template

**Connection request** - blank

**Message 1** (on accept)

> Hey {{first_name}}, guessing infra at {{company_name}} mostly lands on whoever's closest when something breaks, since there's no one who only does infra yet?
>
> Or have you got that covered already?

**Message 2** (follow-up)

> The reason I ask, that's the exact gap we fill, we become the infra owner so your builders stay on the product. One team cut cloud spend around 45% doing it.

**Message 3** (follow-up)

> No worries if it's not a priority {{first_name}}. If it ever is, happy to show you where it'd help most.

## Examples

Different mirrors for Message 1, one per variant:

> Hey {{first_name}}, teams at your stage usually end up with a developer quietly owning infra on the side, a totally different job than the one you hired them for. About right, or have you solved it?

> Hey {{first_name}}, moving fast on the cloud, guessing every deploy and fire still routes through the one or two people who know the setup. Is that where {{company_name}} is at?
