# Question-led

One sharp question about their world, and the whole email turns on it. The lowest-friction reply there is, where even a one-word answer counts.

## The play

The question is the email.

Open on one sharp, specific question about their world ("still handling {{task}} in-house?", "who owns {{outcome}} over there?"), a real question a real person would ask, never a rhetorical or a "did you know". One line of context, and keep it light, because the question already lowered the bar. Follow-ups reframe the question a new way.

## Needs

- A pain sharp enough to name in a single, specific question.
- A clear offer that answers that question.
- Base personalization.

## Subject lines

The question itself, or a natural internal line.

- still doing {{task}} in-house?
- Quick question {{first_name}}

## Touches

Two touches.

- **Touch 1** - the question. 2 to 4 variants, depending on the size of the list.
- **Touch 2** - the follow-up, the question reframed a new way. 2 variants.

## Template

**Touch 1**

> Hey {{first_name}},
>
> {a specific question about their world}
>
> {one line of context}. We {the offer as the answer}, {light proof}.
>
> {the CTA}

**Touch 2** - the follow-up, body only, threads under Touch 1

> {the question reframed a new way} {a lighter re-ask}

## Examples

Real production (decorative plaster):

> Hi {{first_name}}, are cracks in your plaster jobs causing callbacks?
>
> We offer a resin-based plaster that works on all surfaces, installed directly over existing tiles with no demolition, no permits, and no cracking. Your clients save on demo costs, and you eliminate callbacks.
>
> Open to a quick look?
