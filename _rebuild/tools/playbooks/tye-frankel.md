# Tye Frankel

Value first, pitch last. You open with something specific and useful you noticed or made for them, earn the read on that, then land the offer at the very end. The value buys the attention the pitch spends. Use when the offer needs a running start and the list is worth per-lead effort.

## The play

- **Open on the value, not on you** — a specific observation or a thing you made: "pulled up {{company}} and noticed {{specific_finding}}", or "put together a quick {{asset}} for you." Concrete and about them; a generic compliment kills it.
- **One line that makes it relevant** — why the finding or the asset matters to their number, in their words.
- **Pitch at the end, short** — only now the offer, one line, plain: what you do and the outcome, once you have earned it.
- **CTA** — tied to the value you opened with ("want the {{asset}}?"), low friction.
- Never open with the pitch, and never foreshadow value you cannot actually deliver.
