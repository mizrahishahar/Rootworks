# Pre-call asset

Show-up protection through a real asset, deployed in context. The value carries the touch; the reminder rides inside it.

## When
Across the gap between booking and the call, especially a longer one, where a bare reminder would waste the touch.

## The play
Pick the asset from the client's Assets that fits this prospect's situation, and deploy it tied to the outcome, personalised, with one line on why it is worth their minute. It arrives as help, never homework, and only an asset that actually exists in the client's Assets.

## Never
An asset dumped without context; a generic send; an asset the client does not have.

## Example
Two days before the call, using the client's real Cactus Movers case study:

> Before Tuesday, here's the Cactus Movers breakdown - a six-crew shop like yours that went from quotes-in-hours to quotes-in-seconds and now closes 70-93% of qualified jobs. Watch the dispatch part; it's exactly what Sam will map onto how you run.

A real, fitted asset tied to the outcome; the show is protected by value, and the call is pre-sold.
