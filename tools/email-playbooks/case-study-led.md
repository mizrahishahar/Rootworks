# Case-study-led

Proof carries the pitch. Lead with a matched result instead of a claim. Roofer to roofer, SaaS to SaaS: the tighter the match, the harder it lands.

## The play

Let the number earn the right to pitch.

Open on the match so it is believable before they reach it ("worked with a {{niche}} about your size"), then the result in plain numbers, closest to their world. The pitch comes after the proof, never before. Follow-ups bring a second proof from a new angle, then route, then value.

## Needs

- A numbered case study in the brief, matched to the segment by niche or size.
- The result as a real figure; never invent or inflate it. No case, no playbook.
- Base personalization plus, ideally, the matched-client name to reference.

## Subject lines

Lead with the result or a natural line.

- how we got {{peer}} to {{result}}
- {{result}} for a {{niche}} like yours
- Quick question {{first_name}}

## Touches

Two touches.

- **Touch 1** - the proof-led pitch. 2 to 4 variants, depending on the size of the list.
- **Touch 2** - the follow-up, a second proof from a new angle. 2 variants.

## Template

**Touch 1**

> Hey {{first_name}},
>
> We worked with a {{niche}} about your size and {result in numbers}.
>
> {the offer: the same outcome, and how}.
>
> {the CTA: the breakdown, or built for you}

**Touch 2** - the follow-up, body only, threads under Touch 1

> {a second proof from a new angle} {a lighter re-ask}

## Examples

Real production (healthcare privacy screens):

> Hey {{first_name}}, hallway beds don't have to mean a lack of privacy.
>
> Over 800 ERs across the U.S. trust Rolascreen to provide instant, retractable privacy screens that work on demand. Easy to clean, reduce hospital-acquired infections, and cost less than traditional curtains.
>
> Does this sound valuable to your team right now?
