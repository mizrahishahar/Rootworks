## 3. Fazio Pillar Assessment

| Pillar | Rating | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| New Money | Strong | Directly attacks the 40–50% lead-loss problem. Faster quoting = more quotes accepted. Cactus went $2M → $7M. Every owner can translate "leads I'm currently losing" into dollars. |
| Done For You | Weak | MovePlnr is SaaS — the owner still runs their business through it. The platform automates follow-up sequences, generates quotes, and surfaces dispatch, but the operator is still the operator. DFY is the automation layer, not a human team. |
| Risk Reversal | Strong | Permanently free plan up to 10 moves/month, no credit card, no expiry. Paid plans month-to-month, cancel any time. Cactus case study is public, named, verifiable, and extreme. Near-zero entry risk. |

**Overall Viability: Compensatable.** DFY is structurally weak (SaaS, not an agency service). New Money and Risk Reversal are both strong and carry it.

**Compensation strategy:** Frame the automation layer as the work transfer. "The platform follows up automatically. The quote generates in seconds. Dispatch assignments go out through the app. You don't do the work — the system does." This is word trickery — the mechanism is operational efficiency, not literal DFY by a human team — but the framing is accurate: the owner's manual labor is genuinely replaced by the system. Flag in use.

**Word trickery in use:** Yes. The automation layer is positioned as DFY to compensate for the SaaS model. The connection is honest — the system does perform the work that the owner was previously doing manually — but this should not be over-stated. Never use the phrase "we handle it for you" to imply a human team is working the account.

## 8. Open Questions / Flags

1. **DFY word trickery in use.** The automation layer is framed as "the system does the work for you." This is honest but must not over-state human involvement. Cold copy should say "the follow-up runs automatically" not "our team handles your follow-up."

2. **No explicit money-back guarantee.** The free plan and no-contract paid tier compensate, but a performance-based or money-back guarantee would strengthen the offer significantly if Nir is open to structuring one. Ask: *"Would you be willing to guarantee X booked demos from the platform, or offer a 30-day money-back on the first paid month?"*

3. **US vs Canada geography.** The offer and case study (Cactus = Western Canada) resonates strongest with Canadian movers. US outreach may need a US-based proof point added when available. Flag for follow-up as the customer base grows.

4. **Demo CTA vs free-trial CTA.** Current campaign asks for a demo (talking to Sam). The free plan creates an alternative CTA path — "start free, no call needed." The two CTAs should not compete in the same email. Decide per sequence which path is primary.
