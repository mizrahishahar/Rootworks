# Dave.io - Cold Call Script

*For Katie: this is Dave's validated cold-CALL script (built with the calling team, refined by Sean). It is a phone script - do not reproduce its phone phrasing verbatim in LinkedIn or email. Instead, mine it for the substance that carries across channels: the pain framing, the qualifying questions, the pitch structure, the objection responses, and the social proof. It is the fullest single source of how Dave sells.*

---

## Persona targeted
CTO / VP Engineering / VP or Head of R&D / Technical Founder.

## Qualifying questions (reusable across channels)
- Which cloud provider are you using today? (AWS / GCP / Azure)
- Do you have anyone dedicated to infrastructure right now, or does it fall on you and the engineers?
- When something breaks or a deployment needs attention, who picks it up today?
- Where does most of that time go: deployments, things breaking, or security and cost cleanup?
- (Larger teams) How many DevOps cover how many engineers?

## The pain framing (the setup before any pitch)
"Most teams we speak with are stuck handling deployments and putting out fires whenever something breaks. Nobody actually owns the infrastructure, so it keeps pulling the team off the product. So who owns that problem today, or is it just getting absorbed by the team?"

## The pitch (the mechanism)
"Dave becomes your infrastructure owner. Our AI plugs into your tech stack - your cloud, your Git, ticketing, and comms channels - and it only ever reads your cloud, never writes into it, so everything stays secure. On top of that you've got a real human, your remote deployed engineer, who checks and signs off on every change before it ships. Your team gets to ship product instead of getting buried in infrastructure, and you scale output without scaling headcount, at a fraction of the cost."

## Expansion points
1. **A real owner, not a tool** - your engineer owns the infrastructure and is accountable for it, like an in-house hire, without the quarter-million salary.
2. **Safe by design** - nothing touches your systems on autopilot; we only read your cloud, and our engineer approves every change before it ships.
3. **Plug and play** - we connect to the tools you already use and you get value the same day, no months of ramp.

## Objection framework
1. **We handle infrastructure ourselves** - agree (a lot of teams do) → clarify (is it pulling your engineers off the product?) → redirect (worth 15 min to see how we take it off their plate).
2. **Not comfortable with AI in our infrastructure** - agree (the cloud is delicate) → clarify (would it help that we only read, never write, and a human approves every change?) → redirect (that's why we run it as a service, not a product).
3. **We could just use Claude / a copilot ourselves** - agree (you could) → clarify (that's not where your team's expertise is, and you'd own all the cost and risk) → redirect (that's exactly what we take off you).
4. **No time** - agree (infra never sleeps) → clarify (any priorities around freeing engineers or avoiding an expensive hire?) → redirect (worth 15 min?).
5. **Send info** - agree → clarify (focus it on taking infra off your engineers, or the cost side vs hiring?) → redirect (I'll send and check what stood out).

## Social proof
"We work with engineering teams like Monday.com, and we plug into whatever cloud and tools you already run."

## Booking discipline
Never let a positive conversation end without the calendar invite accepted. If they ask technical questions ("what are you using, Claude?"), treat it as a **buying signal** and move toward booking a walkthrough, not a defensive answer.
