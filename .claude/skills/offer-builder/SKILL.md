# Offer builder

The offer reaches you as words: a number, a proof point, a guarantee, a deliverable. We rarely control it, so the job is mostly to make whatever we are handed read as a no-brainer to a cold stranger, using nothing but language, inside fixed borders. When we do have leverage to change the offer, the pillars tell us how.

The cold prospect asks one question: is this worth my response? Your wording answers it.

## What you can do (the usual case)

- **Surface the number.** The quantified gap is the strongest thing you have; put it where it cannot be missed. "10 to 20 sales calls a month" beats "more leads".
- **Phrase the risk reversal in the strongest cold-facing words.** Same guarantee, sharper language: "8 booked calls in 60 days or a full refund" beats "results or your money back".
- **Lead with the proof that fits the segment.** The case study closest to who you are writing to; a roofing result sells roofers.
- **Compress to the no-brainer.** Cut every word not doing work. Length is the enemy of a no-brainer.
- **Frame, do not fabricate.** Choosing which true thing to say first, and how to say it, is your full latitude.

## The borders (never touch)

- **Never invent facts** - no case study, number, or client the brief did not give. If the proof is not in the brief, it does not exist.
- **Never change the terms** - scope, price, the guarantee's conditions, the deliverable. You phrase them; you do not redesign them. "Pay per call" does not become "pay per lead" because it reads better.
- **Never promise what cannot be delivered** - a named asset gets made; a number in the body is real.

## When you can modify it (the pillars)

On the rare offer we control, grade and strengthen against the Fazio three pillars:
- **New Money** - a new result, revenue, or outcome for the decision-maker specifically.
- **Done For You** - the work handled, so the prospect lifts nothing. The separator between a premium offer and a cheap one.
- **Risk Reversal** - the guarantee or performance model that moves the risk off the buyer.

A weak pillar can be carried by a strong one; three weak pillars is not a viable cold offer.

## When words cannot save it

A structurally weak offer, no new result, no proof, no risk reversal, a deliverable they do not value, cannot be wordsmithed into converting; it only produces a polished email that burns the list. Name the specific gap to the operator. Sharpening is for a sound offer being undersold, not a rescue for one that should not ship.

## Output: the offer table

Before any playbook runs, present the offer table, what we are doing with this offer:

**The offer, sharpened** - one line, the offer at its cold-facing best.

| Pillar | Rating | What we surface |
|---|---|---|
| New Money | Strong / Weak / Absent | the result for the decision-maker |
| Done For You | Strong / Weak / Absent | what is handled so they lift nothing |
| Risk Reversal | Strong / Weak / Absent | the guarantee, in its sharpest words |

**Verdict** - shippable as-is, needs sharpening (say what), or not viable (a flag, not a rewrite).
