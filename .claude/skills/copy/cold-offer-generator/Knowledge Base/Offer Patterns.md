# Offer Patterns

Proven cold-traffic offer shapes that have closed millions across CA agencies. Each pattern is a template — a *shape* — not a recipe. Fill in the variables with the specific client's situation.

These patterns work because they hit all three Fazio pillars and answer the prospect's Claim + Guarantee + Evidence sequence in one or two sentences.

---

## Pattern 1 — Outcome + Risk Reversal

The simplest viable shape. Names the outcome and removes the risk in one line.

> *"We [outcome] for [role] at [company type] — guaranteed, or you pay nothing."*

Variants:
- *"We help [niche] add [quantifiable result] in [timeframe] using [mechanism]. Guaranteed, or you do not pay."*
- *"I'll [do X] for your [niche] business and guarantee [outcome]."*

Examples:
- *"We help coaches & consultants add $50,000 in revenue in 60 days using a VSL funnel — guaranteed, or you don't pay."*
- *"I'll book 8–12 qualified sales calls per month for your B2B SaaS — or you pay nothing."*

When to use: when there is a clear quantifiable outcome and a willingness to guarantee.

---

## Pattern 2 — Performance Basis

Eliminates buyer risk entirely. The strongest possible Risk Reversal pillar.

> *"Performance-based [service] for [niche] — you only pay for [unit of result]."*

Examples:
- *"Performance-based lead gen for B2B agencies — you only pay for qualified sales calls that show up."*
- *"Pay-per-client cold email — you only pay when we sign you a client."*

When to use: when the operator has the margin and the throughput to deliver enough volume to make per-unit pricing economical.

---

## Pattern 3 — DFY + Outcome + Time

Stacks all three pillars into one shape. Emphasizes the transfer of work and the time-bound result.

> *"I'll build & launch [the thing] for your [niche] business, run all your [related work], and guarantee [outcome] in [timeframe]."*

Examples:
- *"I'll build & launch a VSL funnel for your coaching business, run all your ads, and guarantee $50,000 in profit in 90 days."*
- *"I'll build & launch a full sales system for your B2B agency, write every piece of copy, run every campaign, and guarantee 10 new clients in 6 months — or you pay nothing."*

When to use: when the scope is broad and the deliverable stack is extensive enough to justify high-ticket pricing.

---

## Pattern 4 — Opportunity Framing (Demand Generation)

When the audience is not yet aware they want the outcome. Sells the *concept* first, then the offer.

> *"Most [niche] don't realize that [opportunity]. We [mechanism] so you [outcome] — without [common frustration]."*

Examples:
- *"Most B2B founders don't realize their LinkedIn profile is the highest-converting sales asset they own. We turn it into a full inbound machine — without you posting daily."*
- *"Most coaches don't realize webinars 5x their close rate vs sales calls. We build the entire webinar funnel for you — and guarantee profit on cold traffic."*

When to use: when the offer requires the prospect to first believe in the underlying mechanism. Common with demand-generation offers.

---

## Pattern 5 — Quantified Result + Mechanism + Time

The most explicit shape. All variables filled.

> *"[Specific number] in [outcome dimension] within [timeframe] for [role] using [unique mechanism]."*

Examples:
- *"$50,000 in new MRR within 90 days for B2B SaaS founders using our cold-email + VSL funnel."*
- *"30 demo calls per month within 60 days for AI consultants using LinkedIn outbound + custom playbooks."*

When to use: when the operator has tight, repeatable numbers from past clients and wants to lead with proof.

---

## Headline shapes (from real CA offers)

Direct phrases that have produced revenue:

- *"$1.2M in revenue across 47 agencies."*
- *"We've spent $6,000/day on Ads & Profitably Acquired 120 Clients Per Month."*
- *"100 calls booked or your money back."*
- *"10 new clients guaranteed — or we work for free."*
- *"From 0 to $50K/mo in 6 months. Without ads."*

Each one is concrete. Numbers. Real time horizons. No vague benefits.

---

## Anti-patterns

- **Pulling punches on the claim.** "More leads" instead of "10 new clients." Cold traffic does not buy vague.
- **Stacking services in the headline.** "Cold email + LinkedIn + content + ads" — reads as a shopping cart. Pick one and go deep.
- **Hedging the guarantee in the headline.** "Possibly results within 30–90 days subject to certain conditions" — kills momentum. Guarantee terms live in the contract.
- **Saying "consulting" or "coaching."** Kills the DFY pillar. The cold prospect is buying a transfer of work, not learning.
- **Mechanism-led on a cold audience.** "Our proprietary [framework]" on an unaware prospect is invisible. Lead with outcome.
- **Numbers that are too small.** "5 leads/month" gets ignored. Numbers must sit in paying-attention distance of the buyer.

## Selecting the pattern

| Situation | Use |
|---|---|
| Clear outcome + willing to guarantee | Pattern 1 |
| Need to maximize cold-traffic response, can run high volume | Pattern 2 |
| Broad scope, premium pricing | Pattern 3 |
| Audience does not yet want the outcome | Pattern 4 |
| Strong proven numbers | Pattern 5 |

Pick the pattern that matches the situation. Then fill in the variables from the client's specifics.
