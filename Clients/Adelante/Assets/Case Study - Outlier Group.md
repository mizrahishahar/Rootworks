## The shape

| | |
|---|---|
| Who | UK, cosmetics & fragrance ecommerce. **1,200-1,500 orders/day.** |
| Before | Support team drowning as volume grew. **~90% of inquiries were tracking-related** - a repetitive queue that blocked the higher-value work: product questions, compliance, ingredients, investigations. |
| Tried first | More staff. Email templates. The AI built into their helpdesk. **All failed.** |
| What we did | Agent wired into policies, warehouse systems, courier logic, order tracking, product info. **Rolled out gradually - started at ~10% of tickets**, raised it as confidence grew. |
| After | **80%+ of all tickets resolved end to end by the agent.** Trustpilot **3.5 → 4.0**. 24/7. Sales rose as the Trustpilot score climbed. |
| Headcount | **Nobody was cut.** Framed internally as a workload tool, not a replacement. The team kept its expertise and got capacity back. |

It is the objection-killer for three separate objections at once: *"we already tried AI"* (they did - it failed), *"it will gut my team"* (nobody was cut), and *"it is risky"* (they ramped from 10%). And the Trustpilot move is public, third-party, and checkable.
