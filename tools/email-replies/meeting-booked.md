# Meeting booked

They picked a time or agreed to one. Convey the meeting is set and start the value bridge.

## When
A time is agreed. The moment the situation flips from booking to protecting the show.

## The play
1. **The invite is sent - ask them to accept it.** That is the confirmation; never ask them to re-confirm the time.
2. **Open the value bridge:** "until the meeting I'll share a few materials so you get the most out of it" - phrased to the real gap (today, tomorrow, next week), framed as for them, the outcome in mind. This sets up Pre-call.

## Never
Re-ask the time; over-thank; a calendar link; the word "locked."

## Example
An owner agreed to Tuesday 2pm:

> Perfect - just sent the invite over, go ahead and accept it and you're set for Tuesday 2pm PT.
>
> Between now and then I'll send a couple of things worth two minutes each, so Sam can spend the call on your shop instead of the basics.

Conveys it is set without re-asking, asks them to accept the invite, and opens the value bridge toward the call.
