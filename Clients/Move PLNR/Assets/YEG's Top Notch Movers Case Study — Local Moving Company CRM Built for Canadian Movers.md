Dale has spent years in the moving industry, including time in larger multi-truck operations. Today he runs his own tightly coordinated local team in Edmonton, focused on quality, reputation, and delivering moves the right way—not on competing for the cheapest quote.

His previous moving software slowed him down more than it helped. Switching between hourly and fixed-rate pricing took too many steps. Multi-stop moves felt fragile. Editing bookings, jobs, or notes was clunky and frustrating. Even simple updates became time-consuming.

Support hours were limited and U.S.-based. The team didn’t fully grasp Canadian seasonality, weather delays, or the day-of realities of local moving. Dale never configured advanced features because getting the basics done was challenging enough.

Meanwhile, the daily workflow operated on a patchwork: dispatching through text messages, paper time sheets from operators, jobs printed for the warehouse each morning, and no clean way to hide customer information while still showing crews what they needed.

## Room to grow—without per-seat penalties or access bottlenecks

Before MovePlnr, adding office users felt expensive and risky. Shared logins made accountability disappear. Keeping track of who changed what was almost impossible.

MovePlnr removes seat limits entirely. Every dispatcher, office member, and call center agent gets their own login—so the entire workflow is traceable and secure. Dale never has to hesitate about giving someone access.

As his team expands from roughly two office staff to five or more, adding users is simple. Roles and permissions scale cleanly, and the CRM remains the single source of truth.
