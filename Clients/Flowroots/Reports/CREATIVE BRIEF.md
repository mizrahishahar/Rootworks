# Creative Brief: Flowroots Performance-Based Lead Generation

## 1. The Offer

### One-sentence proposition
Performance-based lead generation. We book qualified sales calls into your calendar, and you only pay for the ones that show up.

### Quantifiable end result
Qualified sales meetings, booked and shown up to.

### Guarantee (cold-traffic language)
You only pay for qualified meetings that show up. If a meeting doesn't show, you don't pay.

### Deliverables stack

**Infrastructure (built, managed, and yours to keep):**
- Multiple branded sending domains, separate from your main domain
- Dozens of inboxes, scaled to the volume you need
- Full technical setup on every inbox: SPF, DKIM, DMARC and MX records
- A full warmup on every inbox before a single email sends
- Ongoing infrastructure management: deliverability monitoring, bounce control, reputation, and swapping domains and inboxes as they wear

**Leads (yours to keep forever):**
- Identification of your ICP companies from across the entire internet, every business with a live domain, not one saturated database
- Identification of the right decision-maker profiles within each ICP company
- AI research, qualification, and personalization on every lead
- Buying-intent signals layered into targeting
- Email enrichment and verification through a waterfall of 6+ providers
- Multiple ready lead lists of qualified decision-makers with verified emails

**Campaigns:**
- Multiple versions of a cold offer built for strangers
- Multiple cold email sequences and angles, tested across segments and campaigns
- Daily campaign management
- Full inbox management: a dedicated SDR to handle every reply, answer objections, warm prospects, and book meetings into your calendar
- Continuous testing and improvement: proven angles scaled, losing angles cut, lookalike leads sourced from what converts

**Show-up and reporting:**
- Custom pre-call assets (case studies, walkthroughs, FAQs) so prospects arrive warm
- No-show and reschedule follow-up
- Weekly performance reports
- Monthly review calls

### USP / differentiation
Performance-based lead generation. You only pay for qualified meetings that show up.

### Price framing
A monthly tech fee that covers the hard costs of the infrastructure (domains, inboxes, warmup, data sourcing, enrichment, contact verification, and sending tools), plus a flat fee per qualified meeting that shows up. No price figures on the front end. Pricing is a conversation on the call. The headline carries only the result and the guarantee.

## 2. The Audience

### Niche / role
A decision-maker at a B2B company over $1M in annual revenue. Founder, CEO, VP Sales, Head of Sales, CRO, or VP Marketing. Whoever owns the pipeline number. No vertical, no geography.

### Awareness level
Solution-aware but channel-skeptical. They know they need more pipeline. Many have tried cold email and it didn't work, so they doubt the channel or doubt it works for them.

### Current state
Not enough qualified meetings on the calendar. For some that's a maxed-out referral and warm-intro network. For others it's no real outbound pipeline at all. Either way the calendar isn't predictably full, and revenue swings with it.

### Desired state
Qualified meetings show up predictably, with people who fit the ICP and already understand the product before the call. The team closes instead of qualifying.

### Fears and objections
- I've paid agencies before and got nothing.
- It won't work / I won't get results.
- You have no case studies of your own.
- I don't have time to build this in-house or manage another vendor.
- Cold email is dead, won't work in my niche, or will burn my domain.

### Buying triggers
A zero-risk way in: they pay only for qualified meetings that actually show. The incentives are visibly aligned, so choosing us carries no downside.

You get qualified sales meetings on your calendar, predictably, with people who fit your ICP and already understand what you do before the call. We build and run the entire system that produces them. You show up and close. And you only pay for the meetings that actually show, so the risk of trying it is ours, not yours.

## 6. The Gap + How They Bridge It

**The Gap**
They want predictable qualified pipeline. What they have is an empty or inconsistent calendar fed by a maxed-out network. The distance between the two is a working cold outbound system, and they haven't bridged it because doing cold email right is a full discipline: the offer, the infrastructure, the data, the deliverability, the reply handling, the show-up. Their past attempts broke on one of those pieces, so they walked away believing the channel is dead rather than that the execution was off. They don't have the time or the stack to do it properly, and they won't risk another retainer to find out.

**The Bridge**
1. They believe cold email can't work for them, so the offer carries it on performance and they only pay when it works.
2. Their offer was written warm and sent cold, so we build a cold offer from scratch, for strangers.
3. Their emails never reached the inbox, so we run branded domains and warmed inboxes kept separate from their main domain.
4. They reached the same saturated contacts as everyone else, so we identify ICP companies across the entire internet, with intent signals and waterfall verification.
5. They don't have time to run it, so we run the campaigns end to end and an SDR handles every reply.
6. Booked meetings no-showed or arrived cold, so pre-call assets warm every prospect and we chase the no-shows.
7. They were burned by retainers paying for activity, so they pay only for qualified meetings that show.

## 9. The Sales Argument

**Condensed (eight sentences)**

You don't have a consistent pipeline of qualified sales meetings, because referrals have a ceiling and everything else you've tried has failed. The most direct way to fix that is cold email: it has no ceiling, it runs every day straight to people who fit your ICP, and its cost per acquisition sits far below ads. Most of the reasons people think cold email is broken are really execution failures, in the offer, the infrastructure, the data, or the follow-through. We own all of that execution end to end, so all you do is show up and close. We book qualified sales meetings into your calendar, and you only pay for the ones that show up. If a meeting no-shows or you decide it wasn't qualified, you don't pay. Because we're still building our own case studies, we run this risk-free, and that pricing goes away once we have them. So the only question left is whether you believe cold email can work for you, and the guarantee means you don't have to believe it on faith.

**The fifteen pillars**

1. Pick the jury: owner of the pipeline number at a B2B company over $1M, inconsistent or empty calendar, burned on outbound before.
2. State the charge: unpredictable pipeline caps revenue and makes the business impossible to forecast or scale; every empty calendar week is lost revenue.
3. Big Idea + Unique Mechanism: cold email is the uncapped, lowest-CAC pipeline channel; the mechanism is a complete offer-first system run end to end.
4. Establish authority fast: companies running this exact model produce the results in the case studies, and we run the same system on ourselves.
5. Raise the stakes: keep depending on referrals and the swings continue, the forecast stays impossible, and competitors reaching the inbox take the meetings you didn't.
6. Stack proof: concept case studies, aligned incentives, the visible depth of the system, a client testimonial on the model.
7. Chain the logic: you want predictable pipeline; that needs a channel with no ceiling; cold email done right is that channel; doing it right needs this full system; we run it on performance; so this is the logical move.
8. Damaging admission: we have no formal case studies of our own yet, which is exactly why we carry all the risk and run it as a pilot.
9. Detail the offer: the full done-for-you deliverable stack, with you only showing up to the calls.
10. Value stack and price framing: anchor on what one closed client is worth against a monthly tech fee and a per-shown-meeting fee; the ROI calculator makes it tangible (what a client is worth, how many shown meetings to close one, what that makes a meeting cost); price as a range, on the call.
11. Remove risk: performance basis; you only pay for qualified meetings that show; risk is asymmetric in your favor.
12. Add urgency with a reason why: the performance-only pilot exists because we're gathering our first case studies; once they're posted, this pricing goes off the table.
13. Preempt objections: burned before (you pay only on results); no case studies (the guarantee replaces them); cold email is dead (execution, not channel); will burn my domain (separate branded domains); won't work for my niche (if you're B2B, your customer is worth enough, and there are enough companies to reach, yes, and we'll tell you on the call if not).
14. Call to action with micro-steps: book a call; we review your current approach and tell you honestly if it's a fit, what we'd charge, and what's realistic for you.
15. Future-pace ownership: meetings show up predictably, the team closes, the months stop swinging, the pipeline runs without you managing it, and the system is yours to keep.

**The two sales**
Primarily selling the opportunity (that cold email is the channel that fixes inconsistent pipeline), because the audience is channel-skeptical. The facilitator is carried by the guarantee, so the copy spends its energy making them believe the channel works for them, then lets the performance basis remove the risk of choosing us.
