# Email Examples

A library of single emails that pull replies, each chosen to show a different move: a subject family, an opener pattern, a body structure, a CTA type, or a follow-up. Study the move, then make the same choice fresh for your segment. Do not lift a line whole. These are thumb rules, not templates.

Two things to notice across all of them: the opener carries a specific, personalized hook (a real detail about the prospect, often a placeholder the operator fills), and any body with more than one thought is split into two short lines, never a block.

The numbers and client names in these examples are illustrative. In real copy, use only the proof the brief gives you (Principle 9). Never carry an example's number into a live email.

Pick the two or three closest to the segment you are writing. You do not need all of them.

### Direct-question opener + case-study body + catalog CTA
*Segment: medical scrubs to hospitals. Subject family: natural. Body split into two lines.*

Subject: Looking for Figs for your nurses?

Saw {{company_name}} is growing its nursing team - are you sorting new Figs sets for them yet?

We can get you brand new Figs sets at wholesale, so you save 30% on uniform costs a year.
Albany Med saved $50k after making us their supplier.

Would you be open to seeing our full scrubs catalog?

### Personalized status-check opener + short body + "I built you" CTA
*Segment: web design for plumbers. The body is one short line, so it stays one line.*

Took a look at {{company_name}}'s site - are you happy with how many jobs it's booking you right now?

If not, we can build you a new one in 2 weeks.

I put together a sample landing page for you to get a feel for our work. Mind if I share it here?

### Curiosity opener + problem agitation + proof
*Segment: event production for Fortune 500. The opener is a question they cannot answer comfortably.*

Subject: Who owns your lighting rig?

For {{company_name}}'s next event, do you know who actually owns the lighting rig, the LED wall, and the audio?

Most agencies subcontract all of it to vendors who have never met, so when something breaks day-of, nobody owns the fix.
We own it all in-house, 100+ events, zero failures, for brands like Amazon and Kohl's.

Mind if I share a behind-the-scenes reel?

### Follow-up: re-pitch with proof, no re-intro
*A second touch. It never says "following up". Two lines: the proof, then the ask.*

We can get your nursing staff authentic Figs Body Sets at 30% below retail, and we already supply over 50 hospitals, including Albany Med, who saved $50k last year.

Would you be open to seeing our wholesale catalog to compare with your current pricing?

### New-hire opener + responsibility-split body
*Segment: HR / onboarding tech. The opener is a pattern-interrupt off a real firmographic signal.*

How's {{new_hire_name}} settling into the {{role}} seat? Saw you just brought them on.

We can get new hires fully ramped in their first two weeks - we build the plan, run the training, track it, you just point us at the team.

### Unaware-market opener (status-quo as a question)
*Segment: a market that doesn't know the solution category exists. Name their current painful status quo, then introduce the alternative. High-leverage when you have the info.*

Subject: Question about {{company_name}}

Are you still paying a scribe to sit in on your multilingual patient visits?

We built an AI scribe that does it live in any language, so you stop paying per session and your notes are ready before the patient leaves.

I put together a quick demo on a {{specialty}} visit - want to see it?

### Strip-for-trades body (no number, no guarantee)
*Segment: low-sophistication local trade. The niche plus low competition is the differentiator. Plain, no overclaiming.*

Subject: {{company_name}} - quote?

We're a pallet supplier in {{city}} and we'll be running a route past you this week.

Happy to drop off a quote so you can see if we beat what you're paying now.

Want one?

We match brands like yours with influencers who actually convert, then handle the whole partnership.
